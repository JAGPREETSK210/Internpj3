Nvidia and AMD are bringing Microsoftâ€™s Copilot Plus AI features to gaming laptops

Nvidia and AMD are preparing to introduce gaming laptops featuring AI Copilot Plus, a new feature Microsoft recently announced for Qualcomm-powered laptops. At Computex, Nvidia hinted at "RTX AI PC" laptops from Asus and MSI that will later include Copilot Plus features. According to Nvidia's blog post, these Windows 11 AI PCs will get a free update for Copilot+ when it becomes available. This implies that Microsoft may delay the launch of AI-powered Windows features on AMD chips or might offer an exclusive period for Qualcomm-powered Windows on Arm hardware debuting on June 18th. Nvidia is collaborating with Microsoft on AI models integrated into Windows 11, offering developers easy API access to GPU-accelerated small language models (SLMs) that enable retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) capabilities. These AI features, powered by Windows Copilot Runtime, aim to simplify adding AI functionalities to apps using NPU hardware or Nvidia GPUs. Although these AI-enhanced laptops will initially lack Copilot Plus features, a future free update will enable these AI capabilities. Nvidia's launch of an RTX AI Toolkit in June will further optimize and deploy AI models, reinforcing its role in AI-powered computing alongside Microsoft. Author name: Tom Warren
